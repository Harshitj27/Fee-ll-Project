Srinagar

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Srinagar

Top Tourist Places in Srinagar

Shankaracharya Hill

Overlooking Srinagar, Shankaracharya Hill is home to an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Offering panoramic views of the city and Dal Lake, it’s a significant pilgrimage site and a serene spot for spiritual reflection.

Indira Gandhi Tulip Garden

Asia’s largest tulip garden, this stunning attraction in Srinagar bursts into color every spring. Nestled against the backdrop of the Zabarwan Range, it showcases a breathtaking variety of tulips, attracting visitors from around the world.

Dal Lake

The jewel of Srinagar, Dal Lake is famous for its houseboats, shikaras, and floating gardens. A serene and picturesque destination, it offers a unique experience of Kashmiri life on the water, surrounded by majestic mountains.



Sinthan Top

A high-altitude mountain pass connecting Kashmir and Jammu, Sinthan Top offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and peaks. It’s a popular destination for adventure seekers, especially during summer when the road opens for travel.

Doodhpathri

Known as the “Valley of Milk,” Dhoodhpathri is a lush meadow near Srinagar, named for its milky white streams. Surrounded by pine forests and snow-capped peaks, it’s a peaceful retreat for nature lovers and picnickers.

Pari Mahal

Also known as the “Palace of Fairies,” Pari Mahal is a seven-terraced Mughal garden in Srinagar. Perched on a hill, it offers panoramic views of Dal Lake and the surrounding mountains, blending Persian architecture with lush greenery.

Famous Foods of Srinagar

Rogan Josh

A signature dish of Kashmiri cuisine, Rogan Josh is a fragrant lamb curry cooked with yogurt and a blend of spices. Rich in flavor and color, it’s a culinary masterpiece that exemplifies the region’s love for aromatic dishes.

Matschgand

A traditional Kashmiri dish, Matschgand consists of minced lamb meatballs cooked in a rich, spicy gravy. It’s a hearty and flavorful dish, often enjoyed with rice, showcasing the region’s expertise in meat-based cuisine.

Shab Daig

A slow-cooked Kashmiri delicacy, Shab Daig is made with meat and turnips simmered in a rich, spiced gravy. It’s a winter favorite, known for its deep, comforting flavors, often served during special occasions.



Haakh

A staple in Kashmiri cuisine, Haakh is a simple, nutritious dish made from collard greens sautéed with mild spices. It’s a comforting, everyday food, reflecting the region’s emphasis on fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Yakhni

A classic Kashmiri dish, Yakhni is a yogurt-based mutton curry seasoned with fennel and dried mint. It’s a delicate and aromatic dish, enjoyed for its subtle flavors and creamy texture, often served with rice.

Nader Ti Gaad

A traditional Kashmiri dish, Nader to Gaad combines lotus stem (Nader) with fish (Gaad) in a spiced gravy. It’s a unique and flavorful dish that showcases the region’s love for combining local ingredients with aromatic spices.
