Delhi

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Delhi

Top Tourist Places in Delhi

India Gate

India Gate is a grand war memorial in New Delhi, honoring soldiers of the British Indian Army who died in World War I. The 42-meter-high arch, with the eternal flame beneath, symbolizes sacrifice and national pride, attracting countless visitors.

Akshardham Temple

Akshardham Temple in Delhi is a stunning example of modern Hindu architecture. The intricately carved sandstone structure, surrounded by gardens and fountains, showcases India's ancient traditions, spirituality, and culture, drawing visitors for its beauty, exhibitions, and evening light show.

Museum of Illusions

The Museum of Illusions in Delhi offers a mind-bending experience with its interactive exhibits. Visitors explore optical illusions, holograms, and perspective tricks, challenging perceptions and providing a fun, educational adventure that delights both children and adults alike.



National Gallery of Modern Art

The National Gallery of Modern Art in Delhi houses a vast collection of Indian art from the 19th century to contemporary times. The gallery showcases works by prominent artists, offering insights into India's rich artistic heritage and evolving cultural identity.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, a Bahá'í House of Worship in Delhi, is renowned for its flower-like design. Its serene ambiance and open embrace of all religions make it a symbol of unity and peace, attracting visitors for reflection and meditation.

Garden of 5 Senses

The Garden of Five Senses in Delhi is a lush, landscaped park designed to engage all the senses. With themed gardens, art installations, and tranquil spaces, it's a popular spot for leisure, cultural events, and appreciating nature's beauty.

Famous Foods of Delhi

Kebab

Delhi's kebabs are a culinary delight, offering tender, marinated meats grilled to perfection. Influenced by Mughlai cuisine, varieties like seekh and galouti kebabs are rich in spices and flavors, often served with chutney, making them a favorite street food.

Kathi Rolls

Kathi Rolls are a beloved Delhi street food, featuring spicy grilled fillings like chicken or paneer wrapped in a paratha. Quick, flavorful, and customizable with sauces and veggies, they offer a convenient yet delicious meal on the go.

Biryani

Delhi's biryani is a fragrant, spiced rice dish, often layered with marinated meat or vegetables. Influenced by Mughal cuisine, each bite offers a burst of flavors, with saffron, herbs, and aromatic spices, making it a beloved staple across the city.



Samosa

The samosa is a quintessential Delhi snack, featuring a crispy, golden pastry filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. Deep-fried and served hot, it's often accompanied by tangy chutneys, making it a popular, satisfying street food.

Nihari

Nihari is a rich, slow-cooked stew traditionally eaten for breakfast in Delhi. Made with tender meat, usually beef or lamb, and a blend of aromatic spices, it's served with naan or bread, offering a hearty, flavorful start to the day.

Jalebi

Jalebi is a popular sweet treat in Delhi, made by deep-frying a fermented batter into spirals, then soaking them in sugar syrup. The result is a crispy, sticky, and delicious dessert, often enjoyed hot and fresh from the streets.
