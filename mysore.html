Mysore

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Mysore

Top Tourist Places in Mysore

Mysore Palace

A grand royal residence in Mysore, blending Indo-Saracenic architecture with stunning domes, turrets, and arches. Known for its opulent interiors, intricate carvings, and vibrant Dasara celebrations, it’s a symbol of Karnataka’s cultural heritage and royal history.

Somanathapura Temple

A 13th-century Hoysala masterpiece, this temple is dedicated to Lord Keshava. It boasts exquisite stone carvings, intricate sculptures, and a star-shaped layout, reflecting the architectural brilliance and religious devotion of the Hoysala dynasty.

Rail Museum

Mysore's Rail Museum showcases vintage locomotives, carriages, and artifacts, offering a glimpse into India's railway history. Highlights include the Maharani's saloon carriage, a working model train, and educational exhibits that fascinate both children and adults.



Bonsai Garden

A serene escape in Mysore, the Bonsai Garden displays an impressive collection of miniature trees, meticulously shaped and nurtured. It’s a peaceful spot for nature lovers, offering a unique blend of art, horticulture, and tranquility.

Karanji Lake

A picturesque lake in Mysore, surrounded by lush greenery and a butterfly park. It features a large aviary, boating facilities, and a walking trail, making it a popular spot for birdwatching and relaxing amidst nature.

Edmuri Falls

A hidden gem near Mysore, Edmuri Falls offers a peaceful retreat with its cascading waters and scenic surroundings. Ideal for picnics and nature walks, it’s a perfect spot to unwind away from the city's bustle.

Famous Foods of Mysore

Mysore Pak

A traditional sweet from Mysore, Mysore Pak is a rich, buttery delight made from ghee, sugar, and gram flour. Known for its melt-in-the-mouth texture, it’s a must-try delicacy for anyone visiting the city.

Chitranna

A popular South Indian dish, Chitranna is a flavorful rice preparation mixed with lemon juice, turmeric, and seasoned with mustard seeds, peanuts, and curry leaves. It’s a simple yet tangy and refreshing dish, often served as a light meal.

Bisi Bele Bath

A traditional Karnataka dish, Bisi Bele Bath is a spicy, flavorful rice preparation made with lentils, vegetables, and a unique spice blend. Served hot, it’s a hearty and wholesome meal, often accompanied by papad or raita.



Ragi Mudde

A staple in Karnataka, Ragi Mudde is a nutritious ball of finger millet flour cooked in water. Often paired with sambar or curry, it’s a healthy and filling dish, rich in fiber and essential nutrients.

Kosambari

A refreshing salad from Karnataka, Kosambari is made with soaked lentils, grated coconut, cucumber, and seasoned with mustard seeds. It’s a light and nutritious dish, often served as part of festive meals or as a healthy snack.

Kharjoor Halwa

A delectable dessert from Mysore, Kharjoor Halva is made from dates, ghee, and nuts. This rich, chewy sweet is both indulgent and nutritious, offering a unique flavor profile that delights those with a sweet tooth.
