Amritsar

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Amritsar

Top Tourist Places in Amritsar

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, or Harmandir Sahib, is a spiritual and cultural symbol of Sikhism. Its gold-plated structure, serene Amrit Sarovar, and the divine Guru Granth Sahib draw millions of devotees, offering peace, humility, and a sense of unity.

Jallianwala Bagh

Jallianwala Bagh is a poignant memorial to the 1919 massacre, where British troops fired on unarmed civilians. The preserved bullet marks and eternal flame remind visitors of the tragic event that galvanized India's struggle for independence.

The Partition Museum

The Partition Museum in Amritsar captures the pain and resilience of millions displaced during India's 1947 partition. Through artifacts, oral histories, and personal stories, it documents the human cost of division and the enduring legacy of communal harmony.



Ram Tirath Temple

Ram Tirath Temple is a revered Hindu site associated with the Ramayana. Believed to be the birthplace of Lord Ram's sons, Luv and Kush, the temple hosts a sacred tank, ancient huts, and a grand fair, attracting devotees year-round.

Wagah Border

Wagah Border, near Amritsar, is famous for its daily Beating Retreat ceremony. This patriotic display of synchronized marching by Indian and Pakistani soldiers draws crowds, symbolizing the complex relationship between the neighboring nations and their shared history.

Harike Wetland

Harike Wetland, a Ramsar site near Amritsar, is a biodiversity haven. This vast waterbody, created by the Harike Barrage, supports numerous migratory birds, aquatic life, and unique flora, making it a vital ecological zone and a birdwatcher's paradise.

Famous Foods of Amritsar

Amritsari Kulcha

Amritsari Kulcha is a popular Punjabi dish, known for its flaky, stuffed bread filled with spiced potatoes, onions, and herbs. Traditionally baked in a tandoor, it's served with tangy chole (chickpea curry) and chutney, offering a flavorful experience.

Aloo Parantha

Aloo Parantha is a classic North Indian breakfast dish, featuring wheat flatbread stuffed with a spiced potato filling. Cooked with butter or ghee on a hot griddle, it's typically enjoyed with yogurt, pickle, and butter, offering a comforting, hearty meal.

Makki ki Roti with Sarson ka Saag

Makki Ki Roti with Sarson Ka Saag is a beloved Punjabi winter dish. The cornmeal flatbread (makki ki roti) pairs perfectly with the mustard greens curry (sarson ka saag), often served with a dollop of butter, creating a rustic, nourishing meal.



Kulfa

Kulfa is a rich, creamy dessert popular in Amritsar, made from condensed milk, thickened and frozen to create a dense, cold treat. Often garnished with pistachios and saffron, it offers a sweet, refreshing finish to a traditional Punjabi meal.

Dal Makhani

Dal Makhani is a signature Punjabi dish, featuring slow-cooked black lentils and kidney beans in a creamy, buttery tomato sauce. Rich in flavor and texture, it's a staple comfort food, often enjoyed with naan or rice, embodying the essence of Punjabi cuisine.

Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is an iconic Punjabi dish. Tender chicken is simmered in a rich, buttery tomato gravy, flavored with aromatic spices. Its creamy, mildly spiced sauce makes it a favorite, often paired with naan or steamed rice.
