Ahmedabad

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Ahmedabad

Top Tourist Places in Ahmedabad

Auto World Vintage Car Museum

Auto World Vintage Car Museum in Ahmedabad showcases a remarkable collection of vintage cars, including Rolls Royces, Bentleys, and more. This museum offers a nostalgic journey through automotive history, displaying the elegance and engineering of classic automobiles.

Jhulta Minar

Jhulta Minar, or the Shaking Minarets, are an architectural marvel in Ahmedabad. These minarets, part of the Sidi Bashir Mosque, uniquely vibrate when one is shaken, reflecting the ingenuity and mystery of ancient construction techniques.

Victoria Garden

Victoria Garden in Ahmedabad is a serene public park, offering lush greenery and peaceful surroundings. It's a popular spot for locals to relax, jog, or enjoy picnics, providing a refreshing escape from the city's hustle and bustle.

Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity, near Ahmedabad, is the world's tallest statue, honoring Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. Standing 182 meters high, it symbolizes national unity and strength, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and an inspiring tribute to India's "Iron Man."



Sabarmati Riverfront

Sabarmati Riverfront is a beautifully developed urban space along the Sabarmati River in Ahmedabad. It features promenades, parks, and recreational areas, offering a vibrant spot for walking, cycling, and enjoying the scenic views of the city and river.

Adalaj Step Wall

Adalaj Stepwell, near Ahmedabad, is a stunning example of ancient Indian architecture. This intricately carved, multi-story structure was built for water conservation and as a resting place, blending utility with artistry in a cool, subterranean setting.

Famous Foods of Ahmedabad

Khakhra

Khakhra is a popular Gujarati snack, made from thin, crispy roasted wheat flatbreads. Often flavored with spices, it's a healthy and crunchy option for breakfast or tea-time, reflecting the simplicity and taste of Gujarati cuisine.

Fafda

Fafda is a traditional Gujarati snack made from gram flour dough, rolled into thin strips and deep-fried. Often served with spicy chutney and fried chilies, it's a crunchy, savory treat enjoyed especially during festivals like Dussehra.

Dhokla

Dhokla is a steamed, savory cake made from fermented rice and chickpea batter. Soft, fluffy, and mildly spiced, it's a popular breakfast or snack in Gujarat, often garnished with mustard seeds, green chilies, and coriander.



Thepla

Thepla is a versatile Gujarati flatbread made from wheat flour, fenugreek leaves, and spices. Soft, mildly spiced, and portable, it's a staple for travel or picnics, enjoyed with yogurt, pickles, or chutney.

Dalvada

Dalvada is a popular Gujarati snack made from ground lentils mixed with spices, shaped into balls, and deep-fried until crispy. Often enjoyed with tea, these fritters are crunchy on the outside, soft inside, and bursting with flavor.
