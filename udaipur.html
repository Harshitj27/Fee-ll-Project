Udaipur

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Udaipur

Top Tourist Places in Udaipur

City Palace

A majestic complex in Udaipur, the City Palace showcases stunning Rajput architecture, with intricately decorated balconies, towers, and courtyards. Overlooking Lake Pichola, it houses museums and offers panoramic views, reflecting the grandeur of Mewar's royal heritage.

Lake Pichola

A serene, man-made lake in Udaipur, Lake Pichola is surrounded by picturesque ghats, palaces, and temples. Boat rides offer breathtaking views, especially at sunset, making it a quintessential experience of the city's romantic charm.

Bagore ki Haveli

A historic mansion on the banks of Lake Pichola, Bagore Ki Haveli is a museum showcasing royal artifacts, traditional costumes, and cultural exhibits. Its evening folk dance performances and puppet shows are a vibrant celebration of Rajasthani culture.



Jag Mandir Palace

Located on an island in Lake Pichola, Jag Mandir Palace is a 17th-century marvel with stunning marble structures, gardens, and courtyards. Known as the "Lake Garden Palace," it served as a royal summer retreat and hosts lavish events.

Doodh Talai Musical Garden

A scenic garden in Udaipur, Doodh Talai offers panoramic views of Lake Pichola and the surrounding hills. Its musical fountain show, set to vibrant lights and music, provides a mesmerizing evening experience for visitors.

Ambrai Ghat

Ambrai Ghats in Udaipur offers serene views of Lake Pichola and the City Palace. It's a popular spot for sunset-watching, photography, and experiencing the city's tranquil beauty, with reflections of historic architecture creating a picturesque atmosphere.

Famous Foods of Udaipur

Mirchi Bada

A spicy Rajasthani snack, Mirchi Bada is a deep-fried treat made from large green chilies stuffed with spiced potato filling, coated in gram flour. It’s crispy, flavorful, and often enjoyed with chutney or as a tea-time snack.

Gatte ki Sabzi

A traditional Rajasthani dish, Gatte Ki Sabzi consists of gram flour dumplings cooked in a spicy yogurt-based curry. It’s a staple in Rajasthani cuisine, offering a rich and tangy flavor that pairs well with roti or rice.

Falooda

A popular dessert in Udaipur, Falooda is a layered treat made with vermicelli, sweet basil seeds, rose syrup, and milk, often topped with ice cream. It’s a refreshing and indulgent dessert, perfect for cooling down on hot days.



Bread Pakora

A popular Indian snack, Bread Pakora is made by dipping bread slices in spiced gram flour batter and deep-frying them to golden perfection. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, it’s a beloved tea-time treat often served with chutney.

Dilkhushal

A traditional Rajasthani sweet, Dilkhushal, also known as Mohanthal, is made from gram flour, ghee, and sugar, flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts. It’s rich, crumbly, and has a melt-in-the-mouth texture, making it a festive favorite.

Balushahi

A classic Indian sweet, Balushahi is a deep-fried doughnut-like dessert with a flaky, golden exterior and a soft, sweet interior. Often garnished with nuts, it’s soaked in sugar syrup, giving it a delightful crunch and sweetness.
