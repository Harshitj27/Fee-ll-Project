Darjeeling

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Darjeeling

Top Tourist Places in Darjeeling

Batasia Loop

A marvel of engineering in Darjeeling, Batasia Loop is a spiral railway track offering panoramic views of the Himalayas. The loop also features a War Memorial, making it a picturesque spot for history buffs and nature lovers.

Japanese Peace Pagoda

Located in Darjeeling, this serene pagoda symbolizes peace and unity. Built by the Nipponzan Myohoji organization, it offers stunning views of the Himalayas and serves as a peaceful retreat for meditation and spiritual reflection.

Tiger Hill

Famous for its breathtaking sunrise views, Tiger Hill in Darjeeling offers panoramic vistas of Mount Everest and Kanchenjunga. It’s a must-visit for early risers and photography enthusiasts, capturing the majestic beauty of the Himalayas.



Ghoom Monastery

One of the oldest Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in Darjeeling, Ghoom Monastery houses a 15-foot-high statue of Maitreya Buddha. It’s a place of spiritual significance, offering tranquility and insights into the region's rich religious heritage.

Sandakphu

The highest peak in West Bengal, Sandakphu offers stunning views of the world’s highest mountains, including Everest and Kanchenjunga. A trekker’s paradise, it’s known for its diverse flora, including rare rhododendrons and orchids.

Dhirdham Temple

A prominent Hindu temple in Darjeeling, Dheerdham Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Its architecture resembles the Pashupatinath Temple of Kathmandu, making it a cultural and religious landmark that attracts both devotees and tourists.

Famous Foods of Darjeeling

Churpee

A traditional Himalayan snack, Churpee is a hard, chewy cheese made from yak or cow’s milk. Popular in Darjeeling, it’s a high-energy treat, loved by locals for its long-lasting chewiness and rich, tangy flavor.

Sael Rotis

A traditional Nepali bread popular in Darjeeling, Sael Roti is made from fermented rice batter and deep-fried into a ring shape. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, it’s often served during festivals and special occasions.

Shaphalay

A Tibetan dish, Shaphalay is a deep-fried bread stuffed with meat or vegetables. Popular in Darjeeling, it’s a savory snack that’s crispy on the outside and tender inside, reflecting the region’s rich culinary diversity.



Gundruk

A traditional fermented leafy green vegetable dish, Gundruk is a staple in Nepali cuisine, widely enjoyed in Darjeeling. It’s tangy and slightly sour, often served as a side dish, highlighting the region’s preference for preserved foods.

Aloo Dum

A popular street food in Darjeeling, Aloo Dum is a spicy potato curry, often served with Sel Roti. Rich in flavor and heat, it’s a comforting dish that reflects the local love for bold, spiced flavors.

Kheer

A beloved Indian dessert, Kheer in Darjeeling is a creamy, sweet rice pudding flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts. It’s a festive treat, often served during special occasions, embodying the warmth of local hospitality.
