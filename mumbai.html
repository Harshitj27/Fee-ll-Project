Mumbai

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Mumbai

Top Tourist Places in Mumbai

Marine Drive

A popular promenade along Mumbai’s coast, Marine Drive offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea, especially at sunset. It's a lively spot for locals and tourists, known for its arc-shaped design and the iconic "Queen's Necklace" lighting at night.

Gateway of India

An iconic monument overlooking the Arabian Sea, built in 1924 to commemorate King George V’s visit. A symbol of Mumbai's colonial history, it’s a must-visit landmark, bustling with tourists, photographers, and boat rides to Elephanta Caves.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus

A UNESCO World Heritage site, this historic railway station in Mumbai is a masterpiece of Victorian Gothic architecture. Built in 1888, it serves as a bustling transit hub and stands as a symbol of the city’s colonial past and modern progress.



Kanheri Caves

Ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves located in the Sanjay Gandhi National Park. Dating back to the 1st century BC, these caves feature intricate carvings, stupas, and prayer halls, offering a glimpse into early Buddhist life and architecture in India.

Bandra Bandstand

A popular seaside promenade in Bandra, known for its scenic views, jogging tracks, and Bollywood celebrity homes. It’s a great spot for evening strolls, with attractions like the Bandra Fort and Mount Mary Church nearby.

Siddhivinayak Temple

A revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha, located in Prabhadevi, Mumbai. This temple attracts thousands of devotees daily, including celebrities and politicians, who come to seek blessings and experience the divine ambiance.

Famous Foods of Mumbai

Vada Pav

Often called Mumbai’s “poor man’s burger,” this is a spicy potato fritter (vada) served in a bread bun (pav). It’s a quintessential street food, loved for its fiery flavors, often accompanied by chutneys and fried green chilies.

Bhel Puri

A flavorful street food sandwich made with layers of sliced vegetables, green chutney, and a sprinkle of chaat masala, often toasted to perfection. It’s a beloved snack, reflecting Mumbai’s blend of traditional and modern culinary influences.

Bombay Sandwich

A beloved Indian street food consisting of hollow, crispy puris filled with spicy tamarind water, potatoes, and chickpeas. It’s a burst of tangy, spicy flavors in every bite, making it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.



Pani Puri

A beloved Indian street food consisting of hollow, crispy puris filled with spicy tamarind water, potatoes, and chickpeas. It’s a burst of tangy, spicy flavors in every bite, making it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Ragda Pattice

A popular Mumbai street food dish made of potato patties (pattice) topped with a spicy white pea curry (ragda), chutneys, and sev. It’s a hearty, flavorful snack enjoyed across the city, especially at chaat stalls.

Akuri Toast

A classic Parsi dish of spicy scrambled eggs served on buttered toast. Flavored with onions, tomatoes, and green chilies, it’s a delicious and comforting breakfast option, showcasing Mumbai’s rich Parsi culinary heritage.
