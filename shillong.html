Shillong

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Shillong

Top Tourist Places in Shillong

Laitlum Canyons

Laitlum Canyons in Shillong offer breathtaking views of rolling hills and deep gorges. Known as the "End of the World," this serene spot is perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers, providing panoramic vistas of Meghalaya's pristine landscapes.

Umiam Lake

Umiam Lake, also known as Barapani, is a man-made reservoir near Shillong. Surrounded by lush hills, it’s a popular destination for water sports and picnics, offering a tranquil escape with stunning sunsets and scenic beauty.

Mawphlang Sacred Forest

Mawphlang Sacred Forest is a protected area near Shillong, revered by the Khasi tribe. It’s a biodiversity hotspot, rich in rare flora, and is believed to be guarded by ancient deities, making it a place of spiritual significance.



Shillong Peak

The highest point in Shillong, Shillong Peak offers panoramic views of the city and its surrounding hills. A popular tourist spot, it’s also a site of religious importance for the locals, with a small temple at the summit.

Mawjymbuin Cave

Located near Mawsynram, Mawjymbuin Cave is known for its natural Shivalinga-shaped stalagmite. This sacred site is a blend of religious and geological wonder, attracting pilgrims and tourists alike to its mysterious and spiritual atmosphere.

Don Bosco Museum

The Don Bosco Museum in Shillong is a seven-story cultural center showcasing the rich heritage of Northeast India. It’s an educational hub, offering insights into the diverse cultures, traditions, and history of the region’s indigenous communities.

Famous Foods of Shillong

Jdoh

A traditional Khasi dish, Jdoh is a flavorful rice dish cooked with pork, turmeric, and local spices. It’s a staple in Shillong, offering a hearty and aromatic taste of the region’s culinary traditions.

Kyat

Kyat is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice, popular in Shillong. It’s a mildly intoxicating drink, often consumed during social gatherings and celebrations, reflecting the region’s rich tradition of homemade brews.

Doh Khlieh

Doh Khlieh is a Khasi salad made from boiled pork, onions, and spices. Sometimes enhanced with a touch of fermented fish or beans, it’s a refreshing and tangy dish that highlights the simplicity of Shillong’s local cuisine.



Tungrymbai

Tungrymbai is a fermented soybean dish, typically sautéed with pork, ginger, garlic, and local herbs. It’s a staple in Shillong, known for its pungent flavor and rich texture, offering a unique taste of the region’s traditional food.

Dohneiiong

Dohneiiong is a Khasi pork curry cooked with black sesame seeds, giving it a distinctive dark color and nutty flavor. Popular in Shillong, it’s a rich, aromatic dish that showcases the region’s love for bold, hearty flavors.

Pumaloi

Pumaloi is a traditional Khasi steamed rice cake, often served as a side dish in Shillong. Made from powdered rice, it’s light, fluffy, and pairs well with the region’s spicy curries and stews.
