Alleppey

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Alleppey

Top Tourist Places in Alleppey

Alleppey Backwaters

The Alleppey Backwaters are a network of tranquil lagoons and lakes, best explored by houseboat. This serene waterway, surrounded by lush greenery and charming villages, offers a unique glimpse into Kerala’s traditional lifestyle and natural beauty.

Krishnapuram Palace

Krishnapuram Palace, located in Alleppey, is renowned for its ancient architecture and murals, particularly the Gajendra Moksha mural. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, this historic temple reflects the cultural and spiritual heritage of Kerala.

Pathiramanal Island

Pathiramanal Island is a small, picturesque island in Vembanad Lake, Alleppey. A haven for birdwatchers, it’s home to a variety of migratory birds, offering a serene escape amidst lush greenery and pristine waters.



Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple

Known for its iconic "Palpayasam" (sweet rice pudding), Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple in Alleppey is dedicated to Lord Krishna. This historic temple, with its exquisite architecture, is a significant spiritual and cultural landmark in Kerala.

Revi Karunakaran Museum

The Revi Karunakaran Museum in Alleppey showcases an extensive collection of art, antiques, and crystal from around the world. It’s a tribute to the legacy of industrialist Revi Karunakaran and offers a glimpse into Kerala’s rich cultural heritage.

Punnamada Lake

Punnamada Lake, part of the Vembanad Lake system, is famous for hosting the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race. This scenic lake in Alleppey is a hub of activity, surrounded by lush landscapes and traditional houseboats.

Famous Foods of Alleppey

Kuttu and Padala Curry

A traditional Kerala dish, Kuttu and Padala Curry combines green bananas (kuttu) with snake gourd (padala) in a spiced coconut gravy. This vegetarian delicacy from Alleppey is flavorful and showcases the region’s love for local produce.

Appam and Stew

A quintessential Kerala breakfast, Appam and Stew is a combination of soft, fluffy rice pancakes (appam) and a coconut-based stew with vegetables or meat. This Alleppey delicacy is light, fragrant, and full of subtle flavors.

Vegetable Thoran

Vegetable Thoran is a traditional Kerala stir-fry made with finely chopped vegetables, grated coconut, and spices. This Alleppey dish is a staple in Kerala’s cuisine, offering a healthy and flavorful side dish.



Kerala Sadya

Kerala Sadya is a grand vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf, featuring an array of dishes like rice, sambar, avial, and more. It’s a traditional meal in Alleppey, especially during festivals, celebrating Kerala’s rich culinary heritage.

Palada Payasam

Palada Payasam is a traditional Kerala dessert made from rice ada (flakes), milk, and sugar. This rich, creamy pudding is a highlight of celebrations in Alleppey, known for its sweet, comforting flavor.

Banana Chips

A popular snack from Kerala, Banana Chips are thinly sliced raw bananas fried in coconut oil and salted. Crispy and addictive, these chips are a beloved treat in Alleppey, often enjoyed with tea or as a festive snack.
