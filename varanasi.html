Varanasi

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Varanasi

Top Tourist Places in Varanasi

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

An iconic Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. It symbolizes spiritual and religious importance, drawing millions of devotees annually, offering a mesmerizing blend of faith, history, and culture.

Dashashwamedh Ghat

The most vibrant ghat in Varanasi, Dashashwamedh Ghat is known for its grand Ganga Aarti. Believed to be created by Lord Brahma, it’s a spiritual hub, attracting pilgrims and tourists for its divine ambiance and rituals.

Assi Ghat

Located at the confluence of the Ganga and Assi rivers, Assi Ghat is a significant site for pilgrims. Famous for its serene environment and the daily Subah-e-Banaras event, it offers a peaceful retreat for meditation and cultural activities.



Ramnagar Fort

Situated on the eastern bank of the Ganges, Ramnagar Fort is a 17th-century structure. It houses a museum displaying royal artifacts, vintage cars, and medieval weaponry, reflecting the grandeur of Varanasi’s history and the lifestyle of its former kings.

Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum

Located within Banaras Hindu University, Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum houses an extensive collection of ancient artifacts, sculptures, paintings, and textiles. It offers deep insights into the cultural and artistic heritage of India, particularly of the Varanasi region.

Lakhaniya Dari Waterfall

A hidden gem near Varanasi, Lakhaniya Dari Waterfall is a stunning natural wonder surrounded by rocky terrain and lush greenery. It’s a perfect spot for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, offering scenic beauty and a peaceful escape.

Famous Foods of Delhi

Baati Chokha

A traditional dish of Uttar Pradesh, Baati Chokha is a flavorful combination of roasted wheat balls (Baati) and mashed, spiced vegetables (Chokha). It’s a rustic, wholesome meal that reflects the rich culinary traditions of the region.

Tamatar Chaat

Tamatar Chaat is a spicy and tangy street food specialty of Varanasi. Made with mashed tomatoes, spices, and crispy namkeen, it’s a unique and flavorful snack that tantalizes the taste buds with its bold flavors and textures.

Malaiyyo

A delicate, frothy dessert, Malaiyyo is a winter specialty in Varanasi. Made from sweetened milk, saffron, and cardamom, it’s known for its light, airy texture and subtle sweetness, offering a unique, melt-in-the-mouth experience.



Launglata

Launglata is a traditional sweet pastry from Varanasi, filled with a rich mixture of khoya, dry fruits, and spices. Deep-fried and soaked in sugar syrup, it has a crunchy exterior and a sweet, aromatic filling that’s a festive delight.

Kachori Sabzi

A popular breakfast dish in Varanasi, Kachori Sabzi features crispy, deep-fried kachoris stuffed with spiced lentils, served with a tangy, spicy potato curry. It’s a hearty and flavorful combination that’s loved by locals and visitors alike.

Laaiya Chana

Laaiya Chana is a savory snack made from puffed rice and roasted chickpeas, seasoned with spices. It’s a crunchy, light, and flavorful treat, commonly enjoyed as a street food snack in Varanasi, offering a burst of taste with every bite.
