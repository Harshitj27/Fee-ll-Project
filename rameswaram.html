Rameswaram

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Rameswaram

Top Tourist Places in Rameswaram

Rameshwaram Temple

The Rameswaram Temple, also known as Ramanathaswamy Temple, is a major Hindu pilgrimage site. Renowned for its magnificent corridors and towering gopurams, it’s one of the twelve Jyotirlinga temples, dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Panchmukhi Hanuman Temple

A unique temple in Rameswaram, the Panchmukhi Hanuman Temple features an idol of Hanuman with five faces. It’s a revered site, where devotees seek blessings, and it’s also known for its floating stone exhibits.

Dhanushkodi Temple and Beach

Located at the tip of Rameswaram Island, Dhanushkodi Temple and Beach are remnants of a town destroyed by a cyclone. The desolate beauty and historical significance make it a hauntingly beautiful place to visit.



Lakshmana Tirtham

A sacred pond in Rameswaram, Lakshmana Tirtham is associated with Lord Lakshmana, brother of Lord Rama. Surrounded by a temple dedicated to him, it’s a significant site for devotees, offering a tranquil atmosphere for prayers.

Kothandaramaswamy Temple

Situated near Dhanushkodi, Kothandaramaswamy Temple is a historic site where Lord Rama is believed to have crowned Vibhishana. The temple, surrounded by the sea, is a serene spot, attracting pilgrims and history enthusiasts.

Thiruppullani

A revered temple near Rameswaram, Thiruppullani is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It’s believed that Lord Rama prayed here before his journey to Lanka. The temple’s architectural beauty and spiritual significance draw devotees from far and wide.

Famous Foods of Rameswaram

Paruppu Payasam

A traditional South Indian dessert, Paruppu Payasam is a sweet lentil pudding made with moong dal, jaggery, and coconut milk. Rich and flavorful, it’s a festive delicacy in Rameswaram, enjoyed during special occasions.

Murukku

A crunchy South Indian snack, Murukku is made from rice flour and lentil flour, twisted into spiral shapes and deep-fried. Popular in Rameswaram, it’s a savory treat enjoyed with tea or as a festive snack.

Karupatti Paniyaram

A sweet delicacy from Rameswaram, Karupatti Paniyaram is made from fermented rice batter and palm jaggery. These soft, fluffy dumplings are a traditional treat, offering a unique blend of sweetness and texture.



Rasam

A tangy, spiced soup, Rasam is a staple in South Indian cuisine, particularly in Rameswaram. Made with tamarind, tomatoes, and spices, it’s often served with rice, offering a comforting and flavorful accompaniment to meals.

Uttapam

A popular South Indian dish, Uttapam is a thick, savory pancake topped with onions, tomatoes, and spices. It’s a common breakfast item in Rameswaram, known for its crispy edges and soft, flavorful center.

Poriyal

A traditional South Indian stir-fry, Poriyal is made with vegetables sautéed in mustard seeds, curry leaves, and coconut. It’s a healthy and flavorful side dish, often served as part of a traditional meal in Rameswaram.
