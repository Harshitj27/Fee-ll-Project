Coorg

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Coorg

Top Tourist Places in Coorg

Abbey Falls

Nestled in Coorg, Abbey Falls is a picturesque waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. The cascading water creates a misty, serene atmosphere, making it a popular spot for nature lovers and photographers seeking tranquil beauty.

Talakaveri

The sacred origin of the Kaveri River, Talakaveri is a revered pilgrimage site in Coorg. Set amidst the Brahmagiri Hills, it features a temple and a holy tank, attracting devotees and offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Tadiandamol Peak

The highest peak in Coorg, Tadiandamol offers breathtaking views and a challenging trek through dense forests and grassy slopes. It’s a favorite among adventure enthusiasts, rewarding climbers with panoramic vistas of the Western Ghats.



Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in the Western Ghats, this sanctuary is home to diverse flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, and rare birds. It’s a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and trekkers, offering rich biodiversity and scenic beauty.

Omkareshwara Temple

A unique temple in Coorg blending Islamic and Gothic architecture, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Its distinct dome and minarets, along with a tranquil water tank, make it a fascinating cultural and religious landmark.

Gaddige Raja's Tomb

A historical site in Coorg, these royal tombs of Kodava kings are set amidst lush gardens. Featuring Indo-Saracenic architecture, the tombs are a peaceful retreat, reflecting the region's rich history and royal heritage.

Famous Foods of Coorg

Kadambuttu

A traditional Coorgi dish, Kadambuttu are steamed rice dumplings, often served with spicy curries. Soft and fluffy, they are a staple in Coorg cuisine, embodying the simplicity and heartiness of local flavors.

Baimbale Curry

A Coorg specialty, Baimbale Curry is made with tender bamboo shoots cooked in a spicy coconut-based gravy. It’s a flavorful dish that highlights the region’s love for earthy, seasonal ingredients and bold spices.

Therme Thoppu

A lesser-known Coorg delicacy, Therme Thoppu is a wild herb curry with medicinal properties. It’s traditionally prepared with local herbs and spices, offering a unique taste and showcasing the region’s culinary connection to nature.



Kaad Mange Curry

A wild mango curry from Coorg, Kaad Mange Curry combines tangy, small mangoes with a rich, spicy gravy. It’s a seasonal favorite, celebrating the region’s abundant natural produce and vibrant flavors.

Akki Payasam

A sweet dish from Coorg, Akki Payasam is made with rice, jaggery, and coconut milk. This creamy dessert is rich and flavorful, often served during festivals and special occasions, reflecting the sweetness of Coorgi hospitality.

Koovaleputtu

A traditional Coorg steamed cake made from ripe bananas, rice flour, and jaggery. Koovaleputtu is a sweet, moist treat, often wrapped in banana leaves before steaming, offering a taste of Coorg’s rustic, home-cooked flavors.
