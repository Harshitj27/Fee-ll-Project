Ujjain

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Ujjain

Top Tourist Places in Ujjain

Bhartrihari Caves

The Bhartrihari Caves in Ujjain are ancient, mystical caves where the poet-saint Bhartrihari meditated. Known for their spiritual aura and historic significance, these caves offer a peaceful retreat for pilgrims and history enthusiasts alike.

Shree Mahakaleshwar Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas, Shree Mahakaleshwar Temple in Ujjain is a significant pilgrimage site. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple is renowned for its unique Bhasma Aarti, attracting devotees seeking spiritual solace.

Ram Mandir Ghat

A sacred spot along the Shipra River in Ujjain, Ram Mandir Ghat is a major site for Kumbh Mela rituals. The ghat’s spiritual ambiance is enhanced by the presence of the Ram Temple, making it a revered destination.



Kaliadeh Palace

Kaliadeh Palace in Ujjain is a historical marvel located on an island in the Shipra River. Known for its stunning architecture and Persian inscriptions, the palace was once a center of astrology and astronomy during the Mughal era.

Jantar Mantar

Ujjain's Jantar Mantar is an ancient observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II. It features impressive astronomical instruments, reflecting the city's historical significance in the study of astronomy and its connection to the Tropic of Cancer.

Sandipani Ashram

A revered site in Ujjain, Sandipani Ashram is where Lord Krishna and Sudama studied under Guru Sandipani. The ashram is a significant spiritual and educational center, attracting devotees and scholars interested in Vedic traditions.

Famous Foods of Ujjain

Dal Bafla

A traditional Malwa dish, Dal Bafla is a wholesome meal of baked wheat dough balls served with lentil curry, ghee, and chutney. It’s a flavorful and nutritious dish, reflecting the region’s rustic culinary heritage.

Aloo Puri

A popular breakfast in Ujjain, Aloo Puri consists of spicy potato curry served with deep-fried, fluffy puris. It’s a comforting and satisfying dish, often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions.

Makhan Bada

A traditional sweet from Ujjain, Makhan Bada is a deep-fried ball made from maida and soaked in sugar syrup. It’s a rich, indulgent treat, often served during festivals and celebrations, reflecting the region’s love for sweets.



Chhole Tikki Chaat

A favorite street food in Ujjain, Chhole Tikki Chaat features spicy chickpeas served over crispy potato patties, garnished with yogurt, tamarind chutney, and spices. It’s a flavorful, tangy snack that’s loved by locals and visitors alike.

Kandabada

A savory snack from Ujjain, Kanda Bada is made from ground lentils and onions, deep-fried until crispy. It’s a popular tea-time snack, known for its crunchy texture and spicy flavor.

Poha

A quintessential breakfast dish in Ujjain, Poha is flattened rice sautéed with onions, mustard seeds, and turmeric, often garnished with sev and coriander. Light yet flavorful, it’s a beloved dish enjoyed with a squeeze of lemon.
