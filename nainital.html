Nainital

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Nainital

Top Tourist Places in Nainital

Nainital Lake

A serene, crescent-shaped freshwater lake nestled in the heart of Nainital, surrounded by lush hills. It offers boating and scenic views, making it a popular spot for tourists and locals alike. The lake glimmers beautifully at night.

Naina Devi Temple

A revered Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Naina Devi, located on the northern shore of Nainital Lake. It is a significant pilgrimage site, known for its peaceful ambiance and stunning views of the lake and surrounding hills.

Pangot and Kilbury Bird Sanctuary

A paradise for bird watchers, this sanctuary near Nainital is home to over 580 species of birds. The tranquil forests and scenic trails provide a perfect escape for nature lovers, with sightings of rare Himalayan birds.



Eco Cave Gardens

A network of interconnected caves and hanging gardens offering a unique adventure experience. Located near Mall Road, Nainital, these gardens are known for their natural formations and are popular with families and children exploring the caves.

Snow View Point

A popular vantage point in Nainital offering breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayan ranges, including Nanda Devi. Accessible by a cable car, it's a must-visit spot for panoramic landscapes and a peaceful retreat above the town.

Dorothy's Seat

A charming hilltop picnic spot offering stunning views of Nainital and the surrounding valleys. Named in memory of painter Dorothy Kellet, it's a serene spot for nature lovers and photographers, often accessed by a scenic trek.

Famous Foods of Nainital

Aloo ke Gutke

A traditional Kumaoni dish made from boiled and sautéed potatoes spiced with local herbs and mustard seeds. This simple yet flavorful dish is often enjoyed as a snack or side dish, reflecting the region's rustic culinary traditions.

Bhatt ki Churkani

A hearty Kumaoni black soybean curry made with locally grown bhatt (black beans). Cooked with spices and served with rice or roti, this dish is known for its rich, earthy flavors and is a staple in local households.

Baadi

A nutritious Kumaoni dish made from black gram flour cooked with mustard seeds and buttermilk. It’s a comforting and wholesome dish often paired with rice, offering a taste of traditional, home-style mountain cuisine.



Arsa

A popular sweet dish in Nainital made from rice, jaggery, and ghee. These deep-fried delicacies are often prepared during festivals and special occasions, offering a taste of the region's rich culinary heritage.

Singodi

A traditional Kumaoni sweet wrapped in molu leaf, made from roasted semolina and coconut, flavored with cardamom. This fragrant, bite-sized dessert is cherished for its unique preparation and is often gifted during festivities.

Bal Mithai

A signature sweet from Nainital made of khoya (reduced milk) and coated with sugar balls. This fudge-like treat, often compared to chocolate, is a favorite among locals and tourists, embodying the sweet essence of the region.
