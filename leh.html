Leh

Explore the top tourist spots and taste the famous foods of Leh

Top Tourist Places in Leh

Khardung-la Pass

Khardung La is one of the highest motorable passes in the world, located at 18,380 feet. It serves as the gateway to the Nubra and Shyok Valleys. The pass offers breathtaking views and a thrilling experience for adventure enthusiasts.

Changthang Wildlife Sanctuary

Spanning the high-altitude Changthang Plateau, this sanctuary is home to rare species like the Tibetan antelope, wild yak, and black-necked crane. It’s a remote, biodiverse haven, reflecting the rugged beauty and ecological significance of the region.

Pangong Tso Lake

A stunning high-altitude lake, Pangong Tso is famous for its mesmerizing blue waters that change hues with the sunlight. Spanning India and China, the lake is a popular tourist spot, known for its serene beauty and tranquil ambiance.



Magnetic Hill

A natural wonder near Leh, Magnetic Hill defies gravity, making vehicles appear to roll uphill when in neutral. This optical illusion, set against the stark Himalayan landscape, attracts curious travelers and adds a mysterious charm to the region.

Leh Palace

Built in the 17th century, Leh Palace is a historic nine-story structure offering panoramic views of the town and surrounding mountains. Once the royal residence, it now stands as a testament to Ladakhi architecture and the region’s storied past.

Spituk Monastery

Perched on a hill overlooking the Indus River, Spituk Monastery is a spiritual center dating back to the 11th century. It houses ancient Buddhist artifacts and offers stunning views, making it a significant religious and cultural site in Leh.

Famous Foods of Leh

Thukpa

Thukpa is a hearty noodle soup, popular in Leh, made with vegetables, meat, and spices. It’s a warming, nutritious dish, ideal for the cold climate, offering a comforting blend of flavors and textures that reflect the region’s culinary traditions.

Skyu

Skyu is a traditional Ladakhi stew made with kneaded wheat flour dumplings, vegetables, and sometimes meat. Slow-cooked and rich in flavor, it’s a staple food in Leh, providing warmth and sustenance in the region’s harsh, cold conditions.

Tingmo

Tingmo is a steamed bread, often served alongside stews and soups in Leh. Soft and fluffy, it’s perfect for soaking up the flavors of accompanying dishes. Tingmo is a simple yet essential part of the Ladakhi diet, reflecting local culinary practices.



Chutagi

Chutagi is a traditional Ladakhi dumpling dish, shaped like a bow-tie and filled with vegetables or meat. Cooked in a thick, hearty soup, it’s a comforting and flavorful meal, embodying the rich, rustic flavors of the region’s cuisine.

Chhurpi

Chhurpi is a fermented dairy product, often consumed as a snack in Leh. Available in both soft and hard forms, it’s made from yak or cow milk, offering a tangy, chewy texture. Chhurpi is a nutritious, protein-rich treat, popular among locals.

Chang

Chang is a traditional Ladakhi alcoholic beverage made from fermented barley. Mildly alcoholic and slightly sour, it’s often enjoyed during festivals and social gatherings, reflecting the region’s cultural heritage and the importance of community in Ladakhi life.
